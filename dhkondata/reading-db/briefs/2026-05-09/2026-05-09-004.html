Perplexity Will Forever Be My Go-To Research Tool
Mindstream  ·  May 9, 2026
Key Summary Points
01Perplexity AI was built by ex-OpenAI, Google, and DeepMind founders to fix search: it searches the web in real time, pulls from multiple sources, and summarizes into one answer with citations attached.
02Unlike ChatGPT, Perplexity's citations are real and clickable — addressing a common pain point where AI-generated stats come with broken links or unverifiable sources from 2009.
03Perplexity works best with outcome-plus-evidence queries ("What does current research say about vitamin D and immune function, with sources?") rather than keyword searches.
04Best practices: click through sources (don't just scan), use follow-up prompts to go deeper in one thread, and challenge answers with "Is there conflicting research?" or "What do meta-analyses say?"
05Perplexity described itself when asked directly: "real-time web access and transparent citations enhance accuracy and verifiability compared to traditional chatbots" — it noted its own limitations in historical depth.

Key Takeaways
Perplexity occupies a specific niche: between Google (links without synthesis) and ChatGPT (synthesis without proof) — useful for fact-checking and sourced research, not general reasoning.
The workflow shift is from keyword to outcome-plus-evidence framing — this changes how you prompt, not just which tool you use.
No AI tool eliminates verification — even Perplexity's advice is to click sources, challenge answers, and ask about conflicting research. Critical thinking is still the skill.
For professional research workflows, the value is reduction of friction (tab-switching, broken links) rather than automation of judgment.
The article is Mindstream's newsletter piece — promotional framing, but the practical tips (trace stats to source, stay in one thread, use follow-ups) are genuinely useful heuristics.
