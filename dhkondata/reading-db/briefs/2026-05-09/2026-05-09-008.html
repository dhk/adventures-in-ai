GSK Goes to China for $1B siRNA Deal in Arrowhead's Obesity Lane
Kyle LaHucik · Endpoints News  ·  May 9, 2026
Key Summary Points
01GSK paid $55 million upfront to Suzhou-based SiranBio for rights to SA030 — an siRNA in Phase 1 for obesity — outside Greater China, with up to $1 billion in biobucks milestones.
02SA030 targets ALK7 (activin receptor-like kinase 7), the same target as an Arrowhead Pharmaceuticals obesity drug — SiranBio positions it as a next-generation obesity treatment with "potential to achieve selective fat reduction without compromising muscle mass."
03GSK does not plan to develop SA030 "for the sole purpose of weight loss" — it will test the candidate in cardiometabolic disease patients with chronic conditions affecting the lung, liver, and kidney.
04The deal extends GSK's dealmaking streak in China — recent partners include Hengrui, Hansoh, and Chimagen — reflecting a systematic China licensing strategy for early-stage innovation.
05The ALK7 target represents a convergence bet by multiple companies: if the mechanism works for selective fat reduction, it would differentiate from GLP-1s by preserving lean mass — a major clinical unmet need.

Key Takeaways
ALK7 inhibition as a next-gen obesity mechanism is gaining real traction — two programs (GSK/SiranBio and Arrowhead) targeting the same receptor signals growing competitive interest.
GSK's expansion beyond "sole purpose of weight loss" into cardiometabolic disease is a hedging strategy: if the obesity indication fails to differentiate, broader indications preserve value.
GSK's 4+ China deals is a pattern, not an isolated move — it is systematically treating Chinese biotech as a pipeline sourcing channel, not just a market opportunity.
The "muscle-sparing" claim for ALK7 is the key differentiator vs. GLP-1s — if validated clinically, this becomes a major selling point given GLP-1's muscle loss liability at scale.
siRNA as a modality for obesity is maturing rapidly — the combination of RNAi precision and once-monthly or quarterly dosing potential makes it a credible competitor to injectable GLP-1s.
