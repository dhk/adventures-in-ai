UCB Bets $2B on Candid's T Cell Engager Ambitions
Kyle LaHucik · Endpoints News  ·  May 9, 2026
Key Summary Points
01UCB acquired Candid Therapeutics for $2 billion cash plus up to $200 million in milestones, to access its T cell engager programs for autoimmune diseases including B cell depletion.
02Candid had been planning to go public via reverse merger with Rallybio before unsolicited interest from multiple companies "led toward a broader evaluation," according to CEO Ken Song.
03Song had previously committed that Candid would "prove or disprove" T cell engagers in autoimmune diseases "for the entire industry" — UCB's acquisition validates that ambition at the deal level.
04This is UCB's second biotech acquisition in two months; the industry has signed at least 30 acquisitions so far in 2026, according to an Endpoints tally — a notably active M&A environment.
05UCB's interest reflects "increasing appreciation broadly across the industry on the benefit of T cell engagers for B cell depletion in a variety of autoimmune diseases," per Song.

Key Takeaways
T cell engagers are emerging as a credible modality for autoimmune disease — not just oncology — with Candid positioned as the proof-of-concept bet for the whole industry.
The IPO-to-acquisition pivot (reverse merger → unsolicited offers → sale) is a pattern for well-positioned private biotechs in a hot M&A environment: timing matters.
30+ industry acquisitions in 2026 YTD suggests large pharma is deploying capital aggressively — BD pipelines are active after years of caution.
UCB's two acquisitions in two months signals a strategic urgency — likely driven by patent cliffs or pipeline gaps that need near-term clinical assets.
Ken Song's "prove or disprove for the entire industry" framing creates significant pressure on the program post-acquisition — UCB bought not just an asset but a modality thesis.
