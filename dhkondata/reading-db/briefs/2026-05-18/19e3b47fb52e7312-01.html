California Governor Candidates on Taxes and Climate
Axios San Francisco  ·  May 18, 2026
Key Summary Points
01 Steyer proposes closing corporate loopholes to raise $20B annually; Mahan targets specific loopholes over broad hikes; Hilton wants to eliminate state income tax on first $100K and a flat 7.5% rate above that.
02 Mahan argues broad tax increases backfire because wealthy individuals can move assets or hire accountants to avoid "poorly designed taxes."
03 On climate: Steyer backs clean home energy access; Mahan would fast-track energy and transit-adjacent housing; Hilton focuses on reducing oil imports and improving forest management.
📰 News & Current Affairs
Key Takeaways
All three candidates acknowledge wealthy Californians' ability to leave — but disagree on whether the fix is loophole closure, flat taxes, or deregulation.
Climate approaches span the spectrum from aggressive subsidies to supply-side resource management, reflecting the governor race's ideological breadth.
Top two vote-getters in June advance to November runoff — this series is shaping early positioning for a pivotal California race.
