BeOne's Brukinsa TV Ad 'Clarity' Hit by FDA for Misleading Suggestions
Fierce Pharma  ·  May 18, 2026
Key Summary Points
01 The FDA issued an untitled letter to BeOne Medicines' U.S. subsidiary over a TV commercial for its BTK blood cancer drug Brukinsa, citing "compelling and attention-grabbing visuals" that did not hold up to scrutiny.
02 The FDA characterized the ad as making misleading suggestions about Brukinsa's efficacy or safety profile — a formal regulatory enforcement action against the drug's marketing.
🏥 Healthcare Reading
Key Takeaways
FDA DTC advertising enforcement remains active even amid broader deregulatory pressure — BeOne is a repeat target of FDA ad crackdowns, suggesting pattern-level compliance issues.
The "visuals that don't match claims" enforcement category is increasingly important as pharma marketing uses more cinematic production techniques that can imply efficacy beyond label.
BTK inhibitor competition (Brukinsa vs. imbruvica/calquence) makes DTC advertising a contested channel — regulatory actions here carry commercial weight.
